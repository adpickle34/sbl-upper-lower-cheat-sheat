Exercise Selection
STRETCH = GROWTH
Prioritize the loaded stretch position. Incline curl > standing curl. Overhead ext. > pushdown for the long head. The bottom of the rep is where growth happens.
NEUROMECHANICAL MATCHING
Choose exercises where the target muscle has the best mechanical leverage. Lateral raise = mid delt. Leg extension = rectus femoris. Your body recruits what it can leverage most efficiently.
MACHINES USUALLY WIN
For isolation work, cables and machines are often superior — safer to fail on, no balance tax, consistent tension. Save free weights for compounds where stability is also a training goal.
LENGTHENED PARTIALS
On your last set of isolation exercises, hit failure, then keep going with partials in the stretched range only. ~40% more growth in some studies. Best on: curls, laterals, leg curls, flys.
AVOID FATIGUE OVERLAP
Don't fry your biceps early on Upper A — you need them for rows. Always sequence compounds first, isolations last. Beginners: favor compound until you can feel the target muscle working.
A vs B VARIATION
Vary the exercise, not the pattern. Incline press (A) → flat press or machine press (B). Back squat (A) → leg press + leg extension (B).
Progressive Overload
DOUBLE PROGRESSION — SBL DEFAULT METHOD
1
Pick your rep range. Default: 4–8. Target 6 reps per set.
Incline Press → 3 sets × 6 reps
2
Add reps each session until you hit the top of the range across all sets at the same weight.
6 → 7 → 8 reps → then add weight
3
Add load (smallest available increment), reset reps to the bottom of the range.
+5 lb → back to 6 reps → repeat
4
Track every session. Can't beat last week? Check sleep and calories before blaming the program.
5
Deload every 4–6 weeks or if performance drops 2+ sessions in a row. Cut volume ~50% for one week.
Recovery Rules
💤
7–9 hrs sleep minimum. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep — this is when muscle protein synthesis actually runs. Without it, volume means nothing.
🍗
0.8–1g protein per lb of bodyweight per day. Non-negotiable. Nothing else matters if this isn't hit. 3–5 meals, 40g+ per sitting is fine.
🍚
Carbs matter. High glycogen = lower CNS fatigue = higher motor unit recruitment = more effective reps per set. Don't train fasted if you want max output.
📅
48–72 hrs between sessions hitting the same muscle. The upper/lower structure handles this automatically — don't collapse it by doubling up sessions.
❌
Soreness ≠ growth. DOMS is muscle damage, not a hypertrophy signal. Chase the progressive overload log, not the soreness.
